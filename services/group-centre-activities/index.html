About this support

This service supports participants to engage in facilitated group and centre-based activities that encourage social connection, confidence, and skill development.

This support is delivered with a person-centred approach and can be adapted to each participant's goals, routines, and preferences.

Activity

Tai Chi Therapeutic Movement Program

This structured Tai Chi program is designed to support NDIS participants in developing balance, coordination, body awareness, and emotional wellbeing through gentle therapeutic movement practices.

The program focuses on improving physical confidence, reducing stress, and supporting participants to engage more actively in their community.

Sessions are tailored to accommodate different mobility levels and abilities in a safe and supportive environment.

Participants will learn practical movement strategies that can assist with:

  • Balance and stability
  • Coordination and body awareness
  • Relaxation and stress management
  • Confidence in daily movement
  • Social participation

What this service includes

  • Structured participation in group programs
  • Supportive and inclusive activity environments
  • Facilitated peer engagement and social confidence
  • Routine-based activity planning and attendance support
  • Encouragement of consistent and meaningful involvement

Expected outcomes

  • Improved confidence in group environments
  • Greater social connection and inclusion
  • More regular participation in structured activities
