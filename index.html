About Us

Our commitment to NDIS care

We are committed to delivering respectful, reliable, and person-centred support. Our focus is to help participants feel safe, heard, and supported in their goals, routines, and choices.

What guides our care

  • Respect for dignity, privacy, and personal choice
  • Clear communication with participants, families, and coordinators
  • Support that encourages independence and confidence
  • Safe, culturally respectful, and inclusive service delivery
  • A practical, compassionate approach to everyday support

Rights & Responsibilities

Participant rights and what we ask from everyone

This section is written in plain language and can be expanded later. It is based on standard NDIS care principles, adapted for NDIS Carer.

Your rights
  • To be treated with dignity, respect, and fairness.
  • To receive support in a safe way and to an appropriate standard.
  • To make choices about your supports, routines, and goals.
  • To have your privacy and personal information protected.
  • To communicate your views freely and be listened to.
  • To involve family, carers, advocates, or support people if you choose.
  • To receive services that respect your culture, values, identity, and beliefs.
  • To ask questions, provide feedback, and make complaints without fear of unfair treatment.
  • To seek advocacy support, including an independent advocate.
Your responsibilities
  • Share information that helps us support you safely and effectively.
  • Tell us if your circumstances change or if an appointment needs to be rescheduled.
  • Treat staff and other people respectfully and safely.
  • Provide feedback so we can improve services.
  • Pay agreed fees and charges as set out in your service arrangement (where applicable).
  • Let us know as early as possible if services are no longer required.
How we support inclusion and advocacy
  • We aim to deliver care that respects personal, cultural, spiritual, and community identity.
  • We work to maintain an inclusive and culturally respectful service approach.
  • We support participants to involve advocates and support people of their choosing.
  • If you need help finding an independent advocate, we can help point you to appropriate services.
  • You can also use the Disability Advocacy Finder provided by the Australian Government.

Complaints & Feedback

How to raise a concern

We welcome feedback and take complaints seriously. You can raise a concern with a manager or staff member, or contact us directly by phone or email.

Making a complaint with us

  • You can contact us by phone or email and explain your concern.
  • If helpful, we can assist you to put your complaint in writing.
  • Your information will be handled confidentially where possible.
  • Anonymous complaints can still be made, but it may limit how we follow up.
  • We aim to respond as soon as possible. A more detailed response may take up to 10 days depending on the issue.

NDIS Commission complaints

Anyone can make a complaint to the NDIS Commission about the safety, quality, or standard of NDIS supports and services.

  • Phone: 1800 035 544
  • TTY: 133 677 (ask for 1800 035 544)
  • Interpreters can be arranged
  • National Relay Service is available
